Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my roof has storm damage?

Roof damage from hail or high winds isn't always visible from the ground. Common signs include missing shingles, dented gutters, dark spots where granules have fallen off, and water stains on interior ceilings. Morton Roofing provides free professional inspections to assess the full extent of damage before you contact your insurance provider.

Do I need to file an insurance claim right away after a storm?

Yes — most insurance companies have strict time limits, often one year from the storm date, to file a property damage claim. Waiting too long can result in a denied claim. Our team helps you document damage and submit accurate claims promptly.

What does a public adjuster do compared to a roofing contractor?

A standard roofing contractor estimates repair costs and performs the work. A public adjuster is a licensed professional who legally represents you — the policyholder — to negotiate the financial payout of your insurance claim. Morton Roofing does both, ensuring fair evaluation and quality restoration.

How long does a typical roof replacement take?

Once your insurance claim is approved and materials are delivered, most residential roof replacements take just 1 to 2 days. Larger commercial or multi-phase projects vary, but we prioritize clean, efficient, and safe construction.

What is the first step after storm damage?

Schedule a free inspection with Morton Roofing immediately following a severe storm. Call 331-481-3708 or fill out our online form — we'll assess your property and guide you through the next steps.
